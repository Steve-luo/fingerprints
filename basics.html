Basics of Fingerprints

Details of anatomy

  • Origin from volar pads and dermal papillae
  • First seen about 6-8 weeks gestation
  • Fully formed by 17 weeks gestation
  • Sweat glands in dermis terminate as pores on the ridges (one row per ridge)

    • Non-ridged Skin

    Papillae

    Pattern of ridges

  • Determined by dermal papillae
  • Layer of cells above dermis (inner layer of skin)
  • Formed during first weeks of gestation
  • Thought to be partially genetic in nature
  • Seen as ridges in the epidermis (outer layer of skin)
  • Each ridge has row of sweat pores

    • Destruction of Fingerprints

  • Dermal papillae must be destroyed
  • Dillinger tried to do this with acid

    • Details of pattern types

  • Loops (60-65% of population)
  • Radial (opens toward thumb)
  • Ulnar (opens toward little finger)
  • Has one delta (ridge point near type lines)
  • Type lines (diverging ridges)
